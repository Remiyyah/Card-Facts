Capybara


The adult capybara stands beside its young offspring near a water bank,
where capybaras mainly reside most of the day.

  • Scientific Name: Hydrochoerus hydrochaeris
  • Average Length: 115 centimeters
  • Average Lifespan: 9 years
  • Habitat: Lakes, Rivers, Marshes.

Although they are smaller in size compared to other animals,
capyabras are actually the world's largest rodents! Their average
length ranges from 100 to 130 centimeters. The name "capybara" means
"master of the grass", which is mainly the diet of these herbivores.
